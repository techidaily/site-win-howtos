![](https://images.drivereasy.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/09/img_59bf9f7e18469.png)

 If you’re on Chrome in Windows 10, and you’re seeing this error saying _**Couldn’t load plug-in**_ , you’re not alone. Many users are reporting it. But the good news is you can fix it easily by yourself.

## How do I fix it?

 Here are 4 solutions you can try. You may not need to try them all; just work your way down the list until you find the one that works.

 **Solution 1:[Rename your pepflashplayer.dll file](https://tools.techidaily.com/drivereasy/download/)**

 **Solution 4:[Make sure your Chrome is up-to-date](https://tools.techidaily.com/drivereasy/download/)**

## Solution 1: Rename your pepflashplayer.dll file

 1) On your keyboard, press the   **Windows logo key ![](https://images.drivereasy.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/08/img_59a516b53b983.png)** and **E** at the same time to open Windows File Explorer.

 2) Go to   **C:\\Users\\Your Users name\\AppData\\Local\\Google\\Chrome\\User Data\\PepperFlash** .

Then double-click the folder with the version number.

![](https://images.drivereasy.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/09/img_59bf35ecbef21.png)

## Solution 2: Delete your PepperFlash folder

1) On your keyboard, press the   **Windows logo key ![](https://images.drivereasy.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/08/img_59a516b53b983.png)** and **R**  at the same time to invoke the Run box.

2) Type **%localappdata%**  and press **Enter** .

![](https://images.drivereasy.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/09/img_59bf391c4fa54.png)

 3) Go to **Google/Chrome/User Data** .

 Then right-click **PepperFlash** , then **Delete** .

![](https://images.drivereasy.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/09/img_59bf3a08cc37d.png)

## Solution 3: Stop your Shockwave Flash

 1) On any tab of your Chrome do this: on your keyboard, press **Shift**  and **Esc**  at the same time to invoke the Chrome’s Task Manager window.

 2) Click **Plugin Broker: Shockwave Flash** , then **End process** .

![](https://images.drivereasy.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/09/img_59bf41e6bd055.png)

3) Restart your Chrome and see if the flash works.

## Solution 4: Make sure your Chrome is up-to-date

 This error could be also caused by an old version of Chrome. To make sure your Chrome is up-to-date:

 1) Click the more options button ![](https://images.drivereasy.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/09/img_59bf4673a619b.png) on your Chrome. Then **Help**  \> **About Google Chrome** .

![](https://images.drivereasy.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/09/img_59bf462d860a4.png)

2) Your Chrome should be updated automatically.

![](https://images.drivereasy.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/09/img_59bf45d77ce9c.png)
