## A Complete Fix for Your Device's Hub Port - Conquering Power Surge Mishaps

![](https://images.drivereasy.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/09/img_5b9f732218c35.jpg)

 When you connect a USB device with your Windows computer, if you’re seeing the error saying**Power Surge on Hub Port** , don’t worry; you’re not the only one. We’ve seen many users are reporting this issue. More importantly, you CAN fix it. Read on and find how…

## 3 fixes for Pow Surge on Hub Port

1. **[Run the Hardware and Devices troubleshooter](https://tools.techidaily.com/drivereasy/download/)**
2. **[Uninstall all your USB controllers](https://tools.techidaily.com/drivereasy/download/)**
3. **[Update your USB controller driver](https://tools.techidaily.com/drivereasy/download/)**

### Method 1: Run the Hardware and Devices troubleshooter

 There’s a built-in troubleshooter in your Windows system helping fix your connected devices issue.

 Here’s how to run the Hardware and Devices troubleshooter on your computer:

1. On your keyboard, hold down the**Windows logo key** , then press**R** to bring up the**Run** box.
2. Type**control** and click**OK** .  
![](https://images.drivereasy.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/09/img_5b9f73a44c3ed.png)
3. Click**Troubleshooting** when**View by Large icon** s selected.  
![](https://images.drivereasy.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/09/img_5b9f745c8d6e2.jpg)
4. Click**Configure a device** under the**Hardware and Sound** section.  
![](https://images.drivereasy.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/09/img_5b9f743777268.jpg)
5. Click**Next** .  
![](https://images.drivereasy.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/09/img_5b9f7479dd3a6.jpg)

 The troubleshooter would then starts detecting and repairing problems for your device.When it completes, see if the error disappears.

### Method 2: Uninstall all your USB controllers

 This problem could be also caused by the**USB controllers** on your computer. If this is the case, you can uninstall all your USB controllers and let Windows reinstall them automatically.

Here’s how you can do it:

1. On your keyboard, hold down the**Windows logo key** , then press**Pause** .
2. Select**Device Manager** .  
![](https://images.drivereasy.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/09/img_5b9f75c1733eb.jpg)
3. Double-click**Universal Serial Bus controllers** . Then**right-click** on every device listed there and select**Uninstall device** .![](https://images.drivereasy.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/09/img_5b9f7575e979a.jpg)
4. Once you uninstall all your USB controllers, restart your computer. Once your system reboots, it will reinstall your USB controllers automatically. Then check to see if the error disappears.

 If both the methods above fails to help, you may need to**update your USB controller driver** .

 You can update your USB controller driver either manually or automatically.
