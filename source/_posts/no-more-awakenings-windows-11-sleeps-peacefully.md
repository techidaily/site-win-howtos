## Windows 11 No Speakers Error? Here's How You Can Fix It

![](https://images.drivereasy.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/08/no-audio-output-device-is-installed-win-10.jpg)

 When the sound icon in your taskbar has a red x on it (as illustrated above), your computer won’t play any sound.

 This is usually caused by one of two problems: either Windows has disabled your audio device or your PC doesn’t have the latest correct device drivers installed, and Windows therefore can’t ‘talk’ to the device.

 If you see**No Audio Output Device is installed** when you hover your mouse over the the sound icon, your audio problem is probably caused by one of these issues, and there are three repair methods you can try that have helped other users. You may not need to try them all; just start at the top of the list and work your way down.

1. [**Automatically update your audio device drivers**](#m1)
2. [**Manually uninstall & reinstall the device driver**](#m2)
3. **[Re-enable the device](#m3)**

## **Method 2: Manually uninstall & reinstall the device driver**

 If a driver update doesn’t fix your issue, try to reinstall your audio driver:

 1) On your keyboard, press the**Windows logo + R keys** simultaneously to open the Run box. Then type**devmgmt.msc** and hit Enter to open the Device Manager.

![](https://images.drivereasy.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/10/win11-how-to-open-the-Device-Manager.jpg)

## **Method 3: Re-enable the device**

 To fix the problem, follow these steps (Steps below are for your reference. The specific steps in different system versions may be different. ):

 1) On your keyboard, press the **Windows logo key + R keys** at the same time to invoke the Run box. Type**devmgmt.msc** hit Enter.

![](https://images.drivereasy.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/10/win11-how-to-open-the-Device-Manager.jpg)

 2) Locate the**Sound, video, and game controllers** category in the list.

![](https://images.drivereasy.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/11/win11-Device-Manager-Sound-video-and-game-controllers.jpg)

 If you don’t see this category in your list, continue with step 2a) below. Otherwise, skip to[step 3](#step3) .

 2a) Click the **Action** menu and select **Add Legacy hardware** (if you see only the Help in this menu, click somewhere in the white space around the list (i.e. make sure nothing in the tree is selected), then click the Action menu again)

![](https://images.drivereasy.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/11/win11-Device-Manager-Add-legacy-hardware.jpg)

 2b) Click **Next**  to start the Add Hardware wizard. (If the Add Hardware wizard doesn’t display at all, you can try [Method 3](https://tools.techidaily.com/drivereasy/download/) .)

![](https://images.drivereasy.com/wp-content/uploads/2019/10/Add-Legacy-hardware-2.png)
